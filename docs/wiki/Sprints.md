# Sprint Management

Sprints are fixed time-boxes (typically 1–2 weeks) that help teams deliver work in predictable increments.

---

## Sprint Lifecycle

```
Backlog → [Plan Sprint] → Active Sprint → [Complete Sprint] → Review
                                                      ↓
                                             Carry-over issues → next sprint
```

---

## Creating a Sprint

1. Go to **Project → Backlog**
2. Click **Create Sprint** at the top of the backlog
3. Set the sprint **name**, **start date**, and **end date**
4. Drag issues from the backlog into the sprint
5. Click **Start Sprint** when ready

> You can only have **one active sprint** per project at a time. Multiple future sprints can exist in planned state.

---

## Sprint Planning

The backlog view shows:

- **Active sprint** — top section, currently in progress
- **Future sprints** — planned but not started
- **Backlog** — unscheduled issues

Drag issues between sections to assign them to a sprint. Use story points to gauge capacity — the sprint header shows the total points of all issues in the sprint.

---

## Running a Sprint

During an active sprint:

- The **board** shows all sprint issues across workflow columns
- Team members update issue status by dragging cards
- The **burndown chart** updates automatically as issues are completed
- Daily standups are easy — use the **My Issues** quick filter on the board

---

## Completing a Sprint

1. Click **Complete Sprint** in the board header
2. A summary shows: completed issues, incomplete issues
3. Choose what to do with incomplete issues:
   - Move to the **next sprint**
   - Move to the **backlog**
   - Move to a **specific sprint**
4. Click **Complete**

The sprint is archived and its data is preserved for reporting.

---

## Sprint Reports

After completing a sprint, view the **Sprint Report** in the Reports section:

| Report | What it shows |
|--------|---------------|
| **Sprint Report** | Completed vs. incomplete issues, added/removed mid-sprint |
| **Burndown Chart** | Ideal vs. actual story point burn per day |
| **Burnup Chart** | Scope creep + completion progress |
| **Velocity Chart** | Story points completed per sprint (last 7 sprints) |

---

## Sprint Goals

Set a sprint goal when creating or editing a sprint. The goal is displayed on the board header as a visible reminder of what the team is trying to achieve.

---

## Mid-Sprint Changes

It's normal to add or remove issues mid-sprint:

- **Add:** Drag an issue from the backlog into the active sprint
- **Remove:** Drag a sprint issue back to the backlog (right-click the issue → Move to Backlog)

The burndown chart tracks scope changes automatically.

---

## Story Points

Story points are estimates of effort, not time. Common scales: Fibonacci (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13) or T-shirt sizes mapped to numbers.

- Set points in the **Story Points** field on any issue
- Sprint headers show total points
- Velocity charts show average points per sprint
- Team capacity planning: compare your sprint's total points to your historical average velocity
